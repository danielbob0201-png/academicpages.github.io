### Abstract

Logical reasoning is a cornerstone of intelligence, yet current language models show limited systematic logical reasoning ability. We introduce SynLogic, a framework for synthesizing large-scale, high-quality, and verifiable logical reasoning data. SynLogic employs a novel multi-stage generation pipeline that combines symbolic logic synthesis with natural language generation to produce reasoning chains with verified correctness. Using SynLogic, we generate a dataset of high-quality logical reasoning examples and use it to fine-tune language models. Our experiments demonstrate significant improvements in logical reasoning benchmarks, showing that SynLogic can effectively improve model reasoning capabilities while maintaining faithfulness.

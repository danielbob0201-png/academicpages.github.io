### Abstract

Chart understanding is a critical visual reasoning task for vision-language models (VLMs), requiring models to interpret visual plots and extract structured information. Despite strong performance on general VQA benchmarks, current VLMs struggle significantly with chart-specific questions. We identify and analyze a key perception bottleneck in VLMs for chart understanding. Through systematic analysis, we discover that the bottleneck arises primarily from the vision encoder's limited capacity to recognize fine-grained visual elements such as axes, legends, and data series in charts. We further propose targeted interventions to address these perception challenges, offering a path toward more robust chart understanding capabilities in future VLMs.
